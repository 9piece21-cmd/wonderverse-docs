## 1. Welcome to Wonderverse

Wonderverse is an end-to-end AIGC creation platform built for drama, gaming, and commerce — and any other scenario where ideas need to scale.
With one idea, you can complete generation, editing, and delivery in a single place.

> *Turn an idea into a finished story, video, or campaign — generated end-to-end in a single click.*

### What is Wonderverse

In traditional content pipelines, teams switch between a dozen tools — write scripts in one, generate images in another, do voiceover somewhere else, then loop back for editing and localization. Scattered assets, lost versions, and cross-tool coordination cost more than the creative work itself.

Wonderverse **packs the entire pipeline into one studio**.

- **The foundation** is 7 atomic AI tools: image generation, video generation, super resolution, subtitles & translation, AI dubbing, horizontal-to-vertical, and smart erase.
- **The middle layer** is **Studio** — a node-based infinite canvas where you freely arrange, save, and reuse those tools as workflows.
- **The top layer** is 4 **ready-to-use scenario packages**: AI Drama, Game Highlights, E-commerce Content, and Marketing Videos.

[Diagram: Three-layer architecture — AI Tools below, Studio in the middle, Scenarios above]

### Why Wonderverse

#### 🎯 End-to-end, not fragmented stitching
From script to finished cut on the same canvas — cutting 80% of tool switching.

#### 🌏 Native Chinese / Japanese / English
UI, voiceover, subtitles, and localization speak all three languages natively — no third-party translation needed.

#### 🧩 Scenarios, ready out of the box
No need to learn node orchestration — open AI Drama, E-commerce, and others as templates, and ship your first cut in 5 minutes.

#### 🔁 Assets and workflows compound
Your team's styles, characters, and workflows become reusable — faster every time, and unmistakably yours.

#### 🤝 Built for teams
Canvases share and fork cleanly. Roles are clear. Sized for content teams from 5 to 50 people.

**Recommended entry**:
- 🎯 [AI Drama Generator Agent](#15--ai-drama-generator-agent) — End-to-end pipeline from script to finished episode

- 📐 [Horizontal → Vertical](#12-horizontal--vertical) — Smart vertical reframing from horizontal masters

**Typical workflow**: Paste script → Lock characters in Library → AI storyboard → Batch generation → Multilingual export

### 🎮 Game Studios

**Common pain points**: Hours-long match / livestream footage outpaces human editors. Multi-platform distribution requires endless re-edits. New version marketing assets can't keep up with patch cycles.

## 3. 5-Minute Quick Start

First time in Wonderverse? Depending on what you want to do today, pick one path —

> 🚀 **Want to quickly generate an image or video?** Use **Create** workstation — 3 steps to output
> 🎨 **Want to build a reusable workflow?** Open **Studio** — 5 steps to master nodes and connections

---

### Path 1 · "Create" Workstation in 3 steps

Best for: First-timers, fastest result.

#### Step 1 · Open Create
After signing in, click **"Create"** from the left sidebar.

#### Step 2 · Pick generation type and model
At the top, switch the tab to **Generate**, then choose **Image** or **Video**.
In the Model dropdown, pick the one you want (recommended: Gemini 3.0 / Kling 3.0).

#### Step 3 · Write prompt + Run
- Write your prompt in the description box
- Upload reference images (optional, up to 14 supported — varies by model)
- Pick aspect ratio, resolution, batch size
- Click **Run** — the result appears in the central preview in seconds

> Want to edit? Switch to the **Edit** tab to invoke super resolution, subtitles, dubbing, horizontal-to-vertical, smart erase.

[Diagram: Create workstation main interface]

---

### Path 2 · Build a workflow in Studio

Best for: Understanding the node flow, building complex and reusable pipelines.

#### Step 1 · Create a canvas
After signing in, click **"Studio"** from the left sidebar → **"New blank canvas"**.
Click the project name at the top to rename it.

> 💡 **Don't want to start from scratch?** The same entry has built-in recommended templates — drama, e-commerce, social media simple workflows are one-click open and easy to modify.

#### Step 2 · Add your first node (Image Generation)
1. Click **"+ Add Node"** in the top-left corner — a node picker pops up
2. Select **"Image Generation"** — a node appears on the canvas
3. Write a prompt in the input box, e.g.:
   > *"A British shorthair cat perched on a windowsill at dusk, golden light slanting in, cinematic color grading, 35mm lens."*
4. In the **bottom-left of the input box**, select your image model (all mainstream models supported)
5. Click **▶ Run** on the node

After 10-30 seconds, the generated image appears in the node center.

#### Step 3 · Add a second node (Video Generation)
Click **"+ Add Node"** in the top-left corner again → select **"Video Generation"** → place it to the right of the image node.

#### Step 4 · Connect nodes · Make the image become the first frame of the video
This is Studio's core operation — **drag a connection**.

1. Click and hold the **output port ●** on the right side of the image node
2. Drag to the **input port ●** on the left side of the video node, release
3. A connection appears — meaning **the upstream image becomes the first frame of the downstream video**

> 💡 This is Studio's essence — any two type-matched nodes can be connected with one line.

Then in the video node:
- Write a sentence describing **motion**: *"Cat slowly turns its head toward the camera, fur drifting in the wind"*
- Select a video model in the bottom-left of the input box
- Adjust parameters: **Aspect 9:16 / Duration 5s**
- Click **▶ Run**

[Diagram: Connection demo + node parameter panel]

#### Step 5 · Preview and export
1. Wait about 60-120 seconds for the video node to generate
2. Click the node center to preview fullscreen
3. Satisfied? Click **"Export"** in the top-right corner of the canvas to export all assets (image + video) in one go

🎉 You've completed your first canvas workflow — from an AI image to an AI video using it as the first frame.

---

### What to try next

- Add a **Super Resolution** node after the video for a higher-quality version
- Connect the video to a **Subtitles & Translation** node for multilingual subtitles
- Save this workflow as a template for future reuse (see [Build a Workflow](#21-build-a-workflow))

## 4. Team & Permissions

Wonderverse is built for content teams of 5-50 people with a lightweight but actionable collaboration model.
Create a team quickly, invite members, assign permissions, and share assets — keep your creative pipeline transparent and under control.

### Create a Team

1. Top-right corner of the personal dashboard → **Create Team**
2. Fill in team name + logo (optional)
3. Choose a team plan (see [Billing & Invoices](#28-billing--invoices))

### Invite Members

Go to **Team Settings → Members**:

- Invite by email (up to 50 at once)
- Generate an invite link (with expiration / member cap)
- Connect enterprise SSO via OAuth (Enterprise)

### Roles & Permissions

| Role | Project | Assets | Billing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **🛡 Admin** | Create / delete / full access | Read + write all | ✅ |
| **🎨 Editor** | Create / edit own + team-shared | Read + write own creations | ❌ |
| **👀 Viewer** | Read-only access to shared projects | Read-only | ❌ |
| **🤝 Guest** | Single-project temporary read-only | ❌ | ❌ |

### Project Sharing

Three visibility levels per project:

- 🔒 **Private** — Only the creator can see it
- 👥 **Team** — Team members access per their roles
- 🌐 **Public link** — Anyone with the link gets read-only access (ideal for external review)

### Library

The team's character library, voice library, style library, and template library all live in **Library**:

- Any asset saved by a member can be upgraded to **team asset** for everyone
- Assets support version history and rollback
- Admins can configure approval flow under **Library → Governance** (Enterprise)

## 7. Image Generation

Generate images from text descriptions. Wonderverse's image models are tuned for **e-commerce products**, **drama styles**, and **marketing KV**.

### Capabilities

- ✅ Text-to-image (pure prompt)
- ✅ Image-to-image (reference + prompt)
- ✅ Multi-image fusion (up to 14 refs supported — varies by model)
- ✅ Multiple built-in styles (anime, 3D realism, ink wash, cyberpunk, chibi, etc.)

### Input

| Input | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Prompt (required)** | Text description, up to 1000 characters |
| **Reference Images (optional)** | Up to 14, varies by model |
| **Negative Prompt (optional)** | What you **don't** want |

### Parameters

| Parameter | Options | Default |
|---|---|---|
| **Style preset** | Anime / 3D realism / Ink wash / Cyberpunk / Chibi / Photo realism / Custom | Photo realism |
| **Aspect ratio** | 1:1 · 4:3 · 3:4 · 16:9 · 9:16 · Custom | 1:1 |
| **Resolution** | SD / HD / Ultra HD | HD |
| **Seed** | Any integer; same prompt + seed = same image | Random |
| **Batch** | 1× / 2× / 4× | 1× |

### Prompt formula

A high-quality prompt usually has 5 elements:

```
[Subject] + [Action / state] + [Environment / time] + [Visual style] + [Camera / lighting]
```

**Example**:
> A young woman in kimono (subject), leaning against a cherry tree (action), Kyoto on a spring morning (environment), ukiyo-e style (style), shallow depth of field with telephoto blur (camera)

More tips in [Prompt Techniques](#24-prompt-techniques).

### FAQ

**Q: How many reference images max?**
A: Up to 14 — varies by model.

**Q: Can I lock the same character across images?**
A: Yes — save the character to [Personal Assets](#22-personal-assets) and reference it whenever needed.

**Q: Who owns the commercial rights?**
A: Paid users own commercial rights to their generated images 〔See ToS for details〕.

## 8. Video Generation

Generate videos from text (optionally with first or first/last frame anchors).
This is Wonderverse's core video engine — the powerhouse behind drama, highlights, and marketing video pipelines.

### Capabilities

- ✅ Pure text-to-video
- ✅ Image-to-video (single image as first frame)
- ✅ First-to-last frame (two images, AI fills the middle)
- ✅ Video extension (continue from the end of an existing video)
- ✅ Audio-and-video output (supported by select models, e.g. Kling 3.0 Omni — generates videos with built-in audio)

### Input

| Input | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Prompt (required)** | Describe the shot, up to 1500 characters |
| **Reference Images (optional)** | Up to 4, varies by model |
| **First Frame (optional)** | Lock the first frame |
| **Last Frame (optional)** | Pair with first frame, AI fills middle motion |
| **Negative Prompt (optional)** | What you **don't** want |

### Parameters

| Parameter | Options | Default |
|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | 4s · 5s · 6s · 8s · 10s · 12s (varies by model) | 5s |
| **Aspect ratio** | 16:9 · 9:16 · 1:1 (varies by model) | 16:9 |
| **Resolution** | 720p · 1080p | 720p |
| **Style preset** | Same as Image Generation | Realism |

### Prompt note

Video prompts differ from image prompts: **you must describe motion**.

```
✅ Recommended: Camera slowly pushes in, the girl turns and smiles, hair drifting in the wind
❌ Not recommended: A smiling girl
```

## 19. Meet the Canvas

**Studio** is Wonderverse's main creative theater.
All your "script → assets → editing" used to happen in scattered tool pages;
here, they merge onto **the same infinitely expanding canvas** — connected by you, completed by AI.

### How it differs from traditional creation tools

| Dimension | Traditional tools | Studio |
|---|---|---|
| **Basic unit** | Files, layers, timelines | **Nodes** (one AI capability = one node) |
| **Mental model** | Linear flow: A finishes, hands to B | **Networked collaboration**: multi-branch parallel, instant feedback |
| **Reuse** | Copy-paste templates | **Save workflows**, one-click re-run next time |
| **Collab** | Files emailed back and forth | Share the canvas — everyone sees the full flow |
| **Iteration cost** | Change one thing, redo the chain | Change one thing, only downstream re-runs |

### Three core concepts

#### 🧩 Node
The minimal operation unit on the canvas. Each node wraps one AI capability — "Image Generation", "Smart Erase", "AI Dubbing".
Nodes have inputs and outputs, like LEGO bricks that snap together.

#### 🔗 Workflow
Connect several nodes with lines — that's a **workflow**.
Workflows can be saved, named, reused, shared — your team's true productivity asset.

#### 📦 Asset
What you produce on the canvas — materials, characters, styles, voices, templates —
is stored in the **Library**. Any node can call any asset. Your team's style carries through every project.

| Node | Input | Output | Use |
|---|---|---|---|

| 🎬 **Video Generation** | Text prompt (+optional first frame) | Video | Short clips, scene shots |

> 💡 More node types coming (music gen, long-form video stitching, etc.), all sharing the same interaction logic.

### Three input sources for a node

#### 1. Direct input
Type a prompt or upload a file manually. Most common.

#### 2. Reference upstream node
**Connect from the upstream output port to the downstream input port** — the downstream node automatically receives the upstream result.
> Example: Connect "Image Gen" to "Super Resolution" — the former outputs, the latter upscales.

#### 3. Call from Library
Drag a character, voice, or style from the right asset panel into the node — the node treats it as reference input.
> Example: Drag "Lead A portrait card" into Image Gen → all generated images feature the same character.

### Two tips to use nodes better

#### ⚙ Open the parameter panel for fine control
Defaults work for 80% of cases. In the ⚙ panel, tune:
model version, style strength, aspect, seed, guidance scale, etc.

#### 🔁 Use batch count to "re-roll"
Try the same prompt 4 times, pick the best. Faster than re-running manually, also saves tokens.

### Three steps to your first workflow

#### Step 1 · Drag in nodes
From the left node library, drag in the nodes you need. Place each at its own spot on the canvas.
> 💡 **Tip**: Arrange left-to-right, top-to-bottom, so flow is intuitive.

#### Step 2 · Connect ports
Drag from an upstream **output port** to a downstream **input port**:

[Diagram: Three-node connection]

Type mismatches are visually flagged red.

#### Step 3 · Run with one click
Click ▶ on the final node. The system **traces the chain upstream**:
- Unrun upstream nodes execute automatically
- Already-generated nodes reuse their results (no resource waste)
- Real-time status per node: ⏳ pending → 🟡 running → 🟢 done / 🔴 failed

### Advanced · Branching and merging

Workflows aren't always linear — branches and merges work too:

#### Fork (one-to-many)
A single output can connect to multiple downstreams.
> Example: One source image enters two "Image Gen" nodes with different styles for side-by-side comparison.

#### Merge (many-to-one)
Some nodes accept multiple inputs.
> Example: Three storyboard videos → Video Stitch node → finished short 〔node availability TBD〕

## 24. Prompt Techniques

Prompt is your shared language with AI. A good prompt pushes generation quality from 60 to 90.
This chapter gives you formulas + pitfalls for three node types.

### Universal principles

| Principle | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Specific > Abstract** | "Golden sunlight at dusk" > "good lighting" |
| **Quantified > Adjectival** | "35mm lens, shallow depth" > "cinematic feel" |
| **Less is more** | 6-12 keywords is best — too many dilute |
| **Structured** | Write in "Subject + Action + Environment + Style + Camera" order |
| **Use negation** | Use negative prompt to exclude unwanted elements |

### Image · prompt formula

```
[Subject] + [Action / state] + [Environment / time] + [Visual style] + [Camera / lighting]
```

**Example**:

| Dimension | Example |
|---|---|
| Subject | A young woman in kimono |
| Action | Leaning against a cherry tree |
| Environment | Kyoto, spring morning |
| Style | Ukiyo-e |
| Camera | Telephoto, shallow background, soft light |

Combined:
> *"A young woman in kimono, leaning against a cherry tree, Kyoto on a spring morning, ukiyo-e style, telephoto with soft background blur."*

#### Style keyword cheat sheet
- **Photo realism**: `photo realistic, 35mm, sharp focus, natural light`
- **Anime**: `anime style, cel shading, vibrant colors, expressive eyes`
- **3D realism**: `3D render, octane, soft shadows, subsurface scattering`
- **Cyberpunk**: `cyberpunk, neon, rain, reflection, futuristic`
- **Ink wash**: `ink wash painting, traditional Chinese, minimalist, monochrome`

### Video · prompt formula

The key difference from image prompts: **you must describe motion**.

```
[Subject + state] + [Subject motion] + [Camera motion] + [Environment mood] + [Style]
```

**Example**:

| Dimension | Example |
|---|---|
| Subject | An elderly man sitting in a convenience store |
| State | Reading the newspaper, intently |
| Subject motion | Slowly turns pages, occasionally looks up |
| Camera motion | Camera slowly pushes from wide to medium |
| Environment | Rainy night, glass reflection, neon outside |
| Style | Japanese cinematic, warm tones |

#### Video prompt pitfalls

| ❌ Not recommended | ✅ Recommended |
|---|---|
| A smiling young woman | She slowly turns her head, revealing a smile |
| Beautiful landscape | Camera pans across snowy mountains and lake |
| Old man reading | He turns pages, distant traffic in the background |

### Dubbing · prompt techniques

The text itself is the prompt, but a few details lift the result dramatically:

#### 1. Tag emotion
```
[joy] "So you're here too!"
[sad, slow] "I thought… I'd never see you again…"
```

#### 2. Tag pauses
```
"That year— (pause 1s) —I was still young."
```

#### 3. Multi-character dialogue
```
A: Why did you come back?
B: (silent) … Because I promised her.
```

### Drama storyboard · advanced prompt

In drama storyboards, prompts must also speak **cinematic language**:

| Shot type | Prompt keywords |
|---|---|
| Wide establishing | `wide establishing shot, sweeping landscape` |
| Medium | `medium shot, waist-up, conversational` |
| Close-up | `close-up, emotional detail, shallow depth of field` |
| POV | `POV shot, first person view` |
| Tracking | `tracking shot, follow subject` |
| Dolly | `dolly in / dolly out, dramatic reveal` |

### Chinese → Japanese

**Language style**:
- Subjects often omitted (first person rare)
- Sentence-end honorifics (です／ます) fit business contexts
- Drama favors plain form だ with rich onomatopoeia

**Voice picks**:
- Drama female lead → "Bright / energetic" voice
- Commercial → "Intellectual / steady" voice
- Avoid "translation flavor" — have a native review the translation first

**Subtitle style**:
- Font priorities: `Yu Mincho`, `Hiragino Sans`, `Source Han Sans JP`
- Line length max 16 full-width chars
- Avoid mixed horizontal English

### Chinese → English

**Language style**:
- Active voice preferred
- Short sentences + rhythm (good for fast-cut shorts)
- Cultural references need **equivalent translation**, not literal

**Voice picks**:
- US voice: young / clear / dynamic
- UK voice: steady / premium / suitable for luxury
- International neutral: commercials all-purpose

**Subtitle style**:
- Fonts: `Inter`, `Helvetica`, `Roboto`
- Line length max 42 chars (with spaces)
- No all-caps (except titles / CTAs)

### Chinese → Korean

**Language style**:
- Strict honorific levels (titles between drama characters matter)
- Short dramas favor direct emotional expression

**Voice picks**:
- Drama → Korean sweet / sharp voices
- Commercial → standard Seoul accent

**Subtitle style**:
- Fonts: `Noto Sans KR`, `Apple SD Gothic`
- Line width similar to Japanese

#### E-commerce style pitfalls

- ❌ Don't let AI modify the product (color / shape / logo) — only the scene
- ❌ Don't use over-aggressive filters — premium feel gets lost
- ✅ 1:1 main image + 3:4 scene image is the golden combo
- ✅ Cross-border e-commerce: ship at least 3 regional model variants (Asia / Europe / America)

### Marketing video styles

| Style | Best for | Signature |
|---|---|---|
| 🔥 **Hype** | Gaming / sports / EVs | Fast cuts + heavy bass BGM + high-contrast colors |
| 💞 **Soothing** | Beauty / mom-and-baby / tea | Slow motion + piano / folk + warm tones |
| 🎉 **Humorous** | Food / daily goods / campus | Twist + rhythm + bright colors |
| 💎 **Luxury** | Luxury goods / jewelry / premium hotels | Slow motion + minimal composition + premium gray |
| 🏃 **Fast-paced** | Live commerce / flash sales | Half-second cuts + bold subtitles + tight BGM |

#### Marketing video · 3-second rule

Platform algorithms decide push within the first 3 seconds. **The first frame must be a hook**:

- ✅ Extreme contrast (iceberg vs flame)
- ✅ Counterintuitive line ("I don't want you to buy this jacket")
- ✅ Strong emotion (laughter / tears / surprise / anger)
- ❌ Slow logo reveal (except luxury)

## 27. FAQ

This chapter compiles the 30 most frequent questions, by category. If yours isn't here, please reach out to [Contact Us](#29-contact-us).

### Accounts & entitlements

**Q: Can multiple people share an account?**
A: Not recommended. Create a team and invite members — each gets their own account.

**Q: How do I upgrade to Enterprise?**
A: Top-right corner → Upgrade → Pick Team / Enterprise 〔Flow TBD〕.

**Q: What's the free quota?**
A: New accounts get 1,000 credits 〔TBD〕, enough for ~30 images / 5 video clips.

### Generation quality

**Q: What if I'm not satisfied with the result?**
A: Try in order:
1. Edit the prompt
2. Tune parameters (raise resolution / change style preset)
3. Add reference images
4. Use batch count (2× / 4×)
5. Use [Super Resolution](#9-super-resolution) for post-processing

**Q: My character keeps changing — what do I do?**
A: Save the character to [Personal Assets](#22-personal-assets), then reference `@character:name` in prompts.

**Q: Video motion is too stiff or too aggressive?**
A: Adjust the "Motion intensity" parameter in [Video Generation](#8-video-generation).

### Latency

**Q: Why is the queue so long?**
A: Peak hours (8 PM - midnight) have longer queues — enable **overnight batch** to let the Agent schedule.

**Q: Can batch jobs run in the background?**
A: Yes — after submission, you can close the page; jobs continue and notify by email when done.

### Collab & permissions

**Q: How do I share a project with an external team?**
A: Project settings → Public link → set permissions and expiration.

**Q: Are enterprise assets encryptable?**
A: Enterprise supports end-to-end encryption and private VPC deployment 〔Details TBD〕.

### Data & copyright

**Q: Will my uploads be used for training?**
A: No by default. Enterprise can sign data-isolation agreements 〔Terms TBD〕.

**Q: Who owns the generated output's copyright?**
A: Paid users own commercial rights 〔See ToS〕.

**Q: Can I delete all my data?**
A: Yes. Settings → Privacy → Delete account permanently removes all data (irreversible after 30 days).

### Billing & invoices

**Q: Do credits expire?**
A: Free credits last 30 days; paid credits follow the subscription cycle 〔TBD〕.

**Q: Can you issue invoices?**
A: Yes. See [Billing & Invoices](#28-billing--invoices).

### Technical

**Q: Browser requirements?**
A: Chrome / Edge latest recommended. Safari may have feature limits.

**Q: Mobile support?**
A: Mobile currently view-only — editing on desktop 〔Mobile editing TBD〕.

**Q: Supported import formats?**
A:
- Images: JPG / PNG / WebP / TIFF
- Videos: MP4 / MOV / AVI / MKV
- Audio: MP3 / WAV / M4A
- Text: TXT / DOCX / PDF (scripts)

- Monthly plans auto-renew by default
- Cancel anytime in **Settings → Subscription**

2. Pick billing month / amount
3. Choose invoice type:

4. Fill in title, tax ID, email / mailing address
5. Invoice issued in 3-5 business days

- Annual plan: prorated monthly
- Credit packs (overage): no refund after sale

- Cross-entity billing 〔Enterprise custom〕
